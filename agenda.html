OUR AGENDA

The biggest challenge we face as veterinary students is the weight of academics,the long lectures and practical sessions,the early mornings and the late nights, the lack of support, and the fear of failure. This burden builds up, and its consequences go far beyond bad grades. I envision a faculty where academic stress turns into academic strength.

ACTION PLAN

  • Advocate for a free week before exams and lecture-free weekends, with reduced clinic rounds, so students can rest, refuel, and regain strength for the week ahead.
  • Relaxation and stress relief programs: Organize faculty-wide relaxation and stress-relief programs such as hangouts, stress-relief talks, and other activities to help us breathe, recharge, and find balance.
  • Launch student-led clubs and professional development such as the Equestrian Club (already in progress) in partnership with,and under the leadership of professionals, Small Animal Club, and Wildlife & Conservation Club. These will give students opportunities to explore our interests, gain practical exposure, and build professional networks before graduation.

The welfare of students is not just a side concern; it's the heartbeat of our association. Believe me when I say: if students are not cared for, nothing else matters. Academic excellence and personal development depend on wellbeing. Many of us face challenges, ranging from academic pressure, financial struggles, lack of support, or even mental burnout. Some conditions can break a student if nothing is done. I believe welfare must go beyond words to action.

ACTION PLAN

  • Emergency Welfare Support System: Seeking for support is normal, asking for help is powerful. I plan to introduce a welfare support scheme where students in need can access relief packs and basic support especially during exam period when the pressure is highest. Through partnership with alumni, sponsors and well-meaning stakeholders.
  • Health and Mental Wellbeing Programs: This will ensure that every student is fit physically and mentally through free medical checkups in collaboration with Human medics, public health to provide personnel and equipment sponsors (labs, pharmacies, supplement companies) to provide essential supplies.
  • Community Outreach Programs: organize faculty-driven community outreach such as dog vaccination drives, dog shows, wildlife awareness campaigns, and other public-health services. These projects will not only rove our relevance to the community but also attract sponsorships, boosting our welfare program which will be reinvested into the welfare of our students.

Veterinary medicine is not just about surviving school; it's about preparing for the future. We don't have to wait until graduation before we discover our passion, build network or create financial opportunities, I believe we can change that starting now. My plan for innovation goes beyond the classroom into the fields where our students can connect directly with big top players in the vet space, clinicians and industry leaders. Through this we will have access to mentorship and internship placement to help guide us into the future.

ACTION PLAN

  • In a bold move to empower our people, during the Jos Breeders conference myself and some other stakeholders engaged the CEO of FarmAlert, Dr Femi Kayode in a strategic conversation to secure a landmark of interest and partnership to support visionary students with scalable investment worthy ideas to gain hands on guidance and connections to become changemakers in the vet space.
  • Student enterprise:
    As part of my vision to building a sustainable innovative enterprise, I would launch a small-scale students-led business venture (PIGGERY) to serve as a financial backbone for AVMS ensuring that we are less dependent on uncertain funding even long after our time here is over. This would Generate income to reinvest directly into student welfare program. After each litter, interested students will be allowed to adopt piglets at subsidized rates or flexible terms. This will provide a chance to start a small-scale farm project of their own, even while still in school. In return, students who adopts a piglet will remit a percentage of their profit to the association. This ensures sustainability and continued support for student welfare initiatives, giving interested students the chance to dream, plan and build wealth with hands on guidance from professionals.

A faculty thrives not only on books and tests but on the bonds we build beyond the classroom, and for way too long we have limited ourselves to the walls of our lecture halls and clinics. My Vision is to create a more socially driven AVMS where students don't just survive the rigor of Vet school but truly connect, grow and belong. Academic advancement goes hand in hand with social development. Through socially driven events that would celebrate our diversity, cultural difference and creativity we'll transform AVMS into a place where memories are made and talents discovered.

ACTION PLAN

  • The books will teach us, but the field will transform us. Organise hangout sessions for every member of AVMS. Picture an association that believes in everyone’s cultural and religious differences. I would love to see an association that celebrates cultural differences from students and staff.
  • I plan to introduce structured inter level activities that goes beyond academics, from sport tournaments, talent showcase, cultural mixers and professional quizzes. These events will boost student morale and give them a chance to learn, compete, play a role to break the barriers between students and create opportunity for true friendship, leadership and team spirit.
  • Promoting equality through inclusive leadership: Leadership should never be distant from the people. To achieve this and make every student feel represented, I'll introduce the appointment of “Presidential Aids” from across different levels to serve as direct link between the executive body and students, bringing forward the challenges and concerns of each level and assisting in implementing student-centred projects and monitoring welfare issues.
  • We deserve an upgrade of our vet week: I will set a new standard for the routine vet week and showcase the pride of our faculty.

For way too long our hard work as students has only been measured by exams and postings, but our growth deserves more than grades. We deserve recognition, celebration and inspiration. That is why I plan to introduce the surgeon's night designed to transform how we see ourselves and our journey.

Why this matters

  • Celebrating our outgoing Doctors; the graduating students will be honoured as the transition into the profession, strengthening our bonds and leaving a legacy to inspire those still on the journey.
  • Recognition of effort; students will be awarded not just for academic excellence but for skills, leadership, innovation, creativity and dedication making hard work visible and rewarded.
  • Networking platform; By inviting alumni, industrial entrepreneurs, this event will create an opportunity for mentorships, sponsorships and connections.
